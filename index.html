Mission & Statement

The Elijah Project exists to make the original source documents of world religions freely accessible — so belief can be examined against primary texts, not secondhand claims.

Misinformation about what religions actually teach runs in every direction, often from adherents themselves. By putting the real texts in one place, side by side with a biblical response, TEP lets truth be verified rather than taken on faith in someone else's summary.

Privacy & Legal

No Accounts, No Personal Data

The Elijah Project does not require an account, login, or any personal information to use. We do not collect names, emails, or identifying data through the app.

What Stays On Your Device

Bookmarks and theme settings are stored locally on your device. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to us or any third party.

Content Updates

When connected to the internet, the app checks for content updates from our content source. This check does not transmit any personal or identifying information.

Source Material

Original texts included in this app are drawn from public domain sources and open licenses. Where a source requires attribution, that attribution is provided within the relevant entry.

Bible text is the King James Version (public domain). The Qur'an translation is supplied by the fawazahmed0/quran-api project, released under the Unlicense (a public domain dedication). Hindu source texts are drawn from openly licensed public-domain translations. A full list of loaded texts and their licenses is shown under Settings → Source Texts.

Texts from other traditions are reproduced as published, without editing, so that each tradition's claims can be examined in its own words.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the update will be reflected here with a revised date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
